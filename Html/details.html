The Most Expensive Car In Carlos Sainz's Collection

BY ALVIN REYES / DEC. 9, 2022 4:23 AM EST

The Formula 1 circle refers to Ferrari driver Carlos Sainz Jr. as the "matador from Madrid." Born Carlos Sainz Vazquez de Castro on September 1, 1994 (via Formula 1), Sainz Jr. is the son of two-time World Rally Champion Carlos Sainz Sr., a man who struck fear in the hearts of professional rally drivers in the early '90s. AutoSport reports that the younger Sainz earned his F1 stripes after winning the Formula Renault 3.5 championship in 2014 as a member of the Red Bull junior racing program. He made his maiden F1 debut with Scuderia Toro Rosso in 2015 alongside Max Verstappen, who is now a two-time Formula 1 World Champion.


But unlike Verstappen and other F1 drivers who habitually amass a drool-worthy car collection, the Spaniard's more modest garage reflects Sainz Jr.'s relaxed demeanor and instinctive passion for speed. In a report by Style, Sainz Jr. said at a press conference for the 2016 Hungarian Grand Prix that he still drives around town in a Volkswagen Golf GTI. Hot Cars adds the VW was a gift from Sainz Jr.'s parents for his 18th birthday, a perfect gift for a hot-blooded teen driver and a car that any 18-year-old wouldn't mind driving.

From VWs to supercars

Carlos Sainz Jr. moved to Renault in 2018 and joined McLaren in 2019 to pair with Lando Norris. Unsurprisingly, his time with McLaren made him rethink the VW as a primary set of wheels. In its place, he added a $300,000+ McLaren 720S and a $250,000 600LT Spider to his garage. However, Sainz Jr. then moved to Ferrari near the end of 2020 and added several prancing horse badges to the growing collection. He acquired an exquisite Ferrari Roma immediately after donning the Tifosi colors — a $250,000 Grand Tourer with a 611-horsepower turbocharged V8 under the hood.

15 Most Expensive Cars Of All Time

A lot goes into developing a car, especially when it comes to the luxury high-performance segment. Because the automotive market is highly competitive, especially when it comes to the top-tier contestants, manufacturers are in a constant race to innovate beyond what they've done before and release only the best of what they come up with. Each top car manufacturer spends millions of dollars on R&D, the high-quality materials used, and on developing the innovative features that make their models capable of high performance. Each of these factors shoots up the final price of the vehicle.
